About me

I'm a master's student in Software Engineering at the University of Tehran, working with Dr. Azadeh Shakery on questions that sit somewhere between Large Language Models, Information Retrieval, and Conversational Recommender Systems. What drives my work is understanding how people express what they want—and how systems can actually listen. I study user intent and personalization through robust modeling, exploring ways to embed LLMs in IR/CRS pipelines that reason, retrieve, and recommend without becoming black boxes. I'm also drawn to the social side: how culture, bias, and fairness shape these systems, and how LLM behavior changes across different contexts. When I work with LLM-based agents, I'm after recommendations that can explain themselves and adapt over time.

Outside academia, I've spent the past four years at Neshan working on search and geocoding for 30M+ users—lifting CTR from 57% to 64%, leading a team, and learning what it means to build systems that actually serve people's needs rather than just technical elegance. I'm always up for conversations about any of this—or adjacent ideas I haven't thought about yet. Feel free to reach out if something here resonates.
